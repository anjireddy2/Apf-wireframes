Site selection

  • Selected site should be in aquaculture zone only.
  • Mangroves, agricultural lands, salt pans, sanctuaries should not select for aquaculture operations.
  • Assessment of quality and quantity of water availability in different seasons
  • Preferably clayey loam soils,
  • Clear access to site,
  • Availability of electricity,
  • Seed productions hatcheries, ice plants, processing plants are to be in the nearest vicinity of selected site are advisable.
  • Skilled manpower availability.
  • CAA norms should be followed.

Soil quality.

  • suitable soils
  • Clayey- loam
  • black cotton
  • Sandy –clay
  • Soil quality parameters
    • pH 6.0-7.5
    • organic carbon 1.5% - 2.0 in 100gm
    • Available nitrogen 50-75mg/ 100g
    • Available phosphorus 3-6mg/100g
    • Calcium carbonate 5%
    • Carbon, nitrogen ratio 20:1

  • Soil pH & recommended dose of lime /ha.

    • Soil pH Agri.lime Quick lime.
    5.5 – 6.0 3 – 5 tons 2 – 4 tons.
    6-6.5 2 – 3 tons 1 – 2 tons.

  • Lime neutralizes the soil acidity and favour to enhance the natural food production in the pond water.
  • Lime will improve the phosphorus availability which is essential for pond productivity.

Pond construction

  • Good farm layout reduces construction cost and easy operation.
  • Ponds should be in rectangular or square shape.
  • Smaller ponds are ideal for better yields as well as good for easy management.
  • 0.25 – 1.0 acre ponds size is good for management operations & better production.
  • Bunds should be firm with slope ratio of 1:2.
  • Minimum depth 1.5-2.0 meters as per the requirement of species cultured.
  • Inlet and out let are to be arranged in opposite direction.
  • Slope of the pond bottom should be towards outlet.
  • In case of shrimp ponds central drain pit also constructed for periodical removal of sludge.

Pond preparation

Drying

  • Drying the pond bottom for 3-4 weeks until cracks appeared.
  • Drying enhances the aeration of soil.
  • Plough the bottom soil up to 10-20 cm depth which encourages organic matter decomposition and oxidation process.
  • Ploughing can be carried out for every 3-5 days period gap.
  • For non-drainable ponds remove the surface sludge up to 10 cm depth and lime application should be done on the bottom and along the inner slope of the bunds.
  • Levelling and compaction of the bottom has to be done.

    • Liming

  • After ploughing soil pH should be tested.
  • To neutralize the soil pH lime should be applied on the bottom and on the slope of the bundhs
  • According to the pH of the soi,l lime doses are determined.

    • Soil pH lime dose/ha
    6.0 700 –1000 kg
    6.5 200 – 500 kg
  • Lime application should be done on wet soil for effective results.
  • If the soil pH more than 8.0--
  • Apply gypsum 1000kg/ ha on the wet surface of the soil and wash with water to reduce pH.

    • Watering the pond

  • Water should be filtered through 60, 80,120 no. mesh bags to avoid the entry of other organisms
  • Filter bags should be long enough for effective and quick filtration.
  • Filter bags should be cleaned before use.
  • Filtered water should be stored in the pond for 48-72 hours.

    • Water culture

  • After 72 hours add water sanitizers like sodium hypochlorite/ bleaching powder @ 1ppm to eradicate any disease causing organisms.
  • While applying sanitizers one should wear gloves and facemasks.
  • After 24 hours of application of sanitizer aerate the water for about 48-72 hours to get rid of the sanitizer effect.
  • Then test the water pH.
  • As per the need apply lime (25-50kg).
  • After 6 days of liming add fertilizers to the pond water.
  • Fertilizers should mix with water and apply.
  • 2nd day of fertilizers application, aerate the water to facilitate uniform distribution of the nutrients.
  • After 4th or 5th day of fertilizer application plankton will develop in the pond water.

    • Water quality check

    • Water pH, transparency ,salinity, alkalinity, hardness, ammonia are key parameters in shrimp farming.
    • If pH should be excess add fermented organic juice to reduce the pH.
    • Fermented organic juice acts as a medium for the growth of useful bacteria as well as improve the carbon nitrogen ratio up to 20:1.

    Shrimp seed selection

    Gross examination

  • Seed colour - dark brown
  • Activeness - swims against water current.
  • Feeding behaviour - readily accept and eat feed.
  • Size variation - uniform or <10% variation.

    • Microscopic examination

  • Muscle - smooth transparent.
  • No. of rostral spines - > 5.
  • Appendages - fully formed, intact with setae.
  • Gut - completely filled with food.
  • Muscle gut ratio in 6th abdominal segment--- 4:1.
  • Carapace length -- more than 6th abdominal segment.
  • Uropod’s - expanded completely.
  • Hepatopatopancreas -- bright yellow colour with oil globules.
  • Fouling --- no fouling appeared on body& appendages.
  • Necrosis --- absent.

    • Stress tests

  • Physical stress test ---Collect nearly 100 PLs from the bottom of the larval rearing tank and put them in a tub and rotates the water. If majority of the PL s are concentrated at the centre of the tub, this indicates the seed is not good.
  • Salinity test --- Collect nearly 100 PLs from the bottom of the larval rearing tank and put them in a tub and rotates the water. If majority of the PL s are concentrated at the centre of the tub, this indicates the seed is not good.
  • Formalin test -- Seed exposed to 100ppm formalin solution for 30 – 60 minutes. Observe the activeness of the seed after one hour. If 90% seed is active select the seed for stocking.
  • Temperature shock --Temperature of the water may raise/ lower the @ 50 C for 30 minute and observe the survival. If survival is >80%, select the seed.

    • Seed stocking

    Density-- 40 - 60 /m2( depends on pond preparation and other conditions).

  • Time of stocking ----- early hours in the morning / evening.
  • Acclimatization ----- seed bags on the surface of the pond for 1 to 2 hours.
  • Seed survival test - seed stocked in hapa within pond, observe the survival at 24 and 48 hours.
  • For low saline water select above PL 12 stages, for high saline waters select above PL 10 stages for better survival.

Feed management

  • Shrimp health and growth depends on feed quality.
  • Freshly feeds should be used.
  • Feed should be having attractive, fishy odour, uniform size, good palatability and water stable for 1-2 hours.
  • Use high protein feed initially to the PL stages up to 30 days.
  • Feed should be broad cast in the pond.
  • Feed should be given at every 4 hours gap during day time.
  • Initially 1.5 kg feed will be given for 1.0 lakh seed. In course of time follow the feed chart according to the age and body weight.
  • After 20 days of culture, put a check tray for assessing the feed consumption.
  • Arrange 6-8 check trays are arranged in four sides of the pond.
  • Feed in the check tray will consume within two hours for first 50 days.
  • After every application of feed check tray observation is must.
  • If the feed in check tray will consume before two hours of time , feed quantity should be increased by 2-5% or if poor consumption ,decrease the feed quantity by 2-3 %.
  • Basing on the feed consumption in check tray, we can assess the feed quantity requirement.
  • Under feeding leads to size variation also.
  • Feeds should not change frequently.
  • For every 1 kg of feed consumption 30 g of ammonia will be added to the bottom in the form of excreta.
  • Quality feed resulting in good FCR.
  • Maintain registers for feed consumption and growth.

Water quality and bottom management

  • After stocking of seed, pond water quality has to be changed due to the metabolic activities of cultured animal
  • pH flu ctuations are common due to the interplay of photosynthesis and respiration. This pH fluctuations will stress the animal. To correct the pH fluctuations liming / water exchange shall be done.
  • Dissolved oxygen is the limiting factor. It will decrease due to over stocking/ excess algal blooms/ heavy rains or exposed to heat waves for longer periods.
  • To maintain optimum DO levels install aerators after 30 days of culture @1 HP per 200- 300 kg of biomass.
  • Long arm and spiral aerators are the best choice
  • Prolonged sunny days affect the metabolism of culture animal. To overcome this problem maintain pond depth and provide aeration.
  • High temperature and high pH of pond water leads to toxic ammonia gas which causes gill damage. To solve this problem reduce feeding and maintain proper algal bloom in pond water.
  • Minerals are essential constituents for skeletal structures ,play a key role in the maintenance of osmotic pressure, vital role in the acid base equilibrium of the body etc.,
  • Mineral concentration should be check for every week.
  • Mineral applications are needed according to the levels of minerals in water. Mineral application is needed for every 10 days in low saline waters(0-5 ppt).
  • Minerals are essential for forming the exoskeleton after moulting.
  • Excess application of minerals spoils the water quality and moulting problems.
  • For shrimp optimum range of calcium, magnesium and potassium are required in the ratio of 1:3:1.
  • Water temperature and pH should always maintain in optimum levels for better growth and survival.
  • Alkalinity is the utmost important water parameter for better animal growth and survival. Alkalinity to resist change in pH of water.
  • Optimum water parameters for shrimp farming

    • Parameter Optimum range.
    DO > 5 ppm
    pH 7.5 – 8.5
    Temperature 28- 32oc
    Salinity 5-28 ppt
    Total alkalinity > 75 – 200 ppm
    Ammonia 750-3000 ppm
    Ammonia <1.0 ppm
    Nitrite <1.0 ppm

  • Periodical application of agri lime and dolomite for correcting the alkalinity, hardness and promotion of plankton growth.
  • Bottom soil should be tested for quality of soil and probiotics application to maintain the clean bottom as and when required.

Health management.

  • Observe the water colour, swimming patterns of the shrimp, feed consumption patterns in the check tray etc. in the morning and evening.
  • Weekly sampling of culture animal for examination of health condition and growth performance.
  • Farmers/ farm managers always observe the activeness, colour of the animal, any abnormal signs the body parts , gills, hepatopancreas and status of gut etc.,
  • After 60- 70 days of culture one should take necessary steps for maintaining the water quality, feed management to minimize the stress on culture animal.
  • Usually mass mortalities happened due to viral diseases.
  • Folded antenna and rostral bent are due to Viral infection ( IHHNV).
  • Round white spots on carapace indicates WSSV.
  • Sometimes reddening of the body, antennal cut, black spots appeared on the body indicates bacterial infection due to water quality deterioration or high densities.
  • Bacterial infections are common in high density culture ponds and also due to feed wastage.
  • Body cramps/loose shells also observed, may be due to the mineral deficiency or animal exposed to prolonged high water temperatures because of shallow ness of the pond or due to heat waves.
  • Any size variation is observed, it may be due to under feeding or microsporidian parasite infection( EHP).
  • Deterioration of bottom quality and high concentration of toxic gases in pond water, presence of harmful planktonic forms leads to brown gill and black gill problems.
  • Harmful planktonic blooms developed in age old ponds after 50-60 days of culture causes the white gut and white faces problems.
  • Animals exposed to low dissolved oxygen for prolonged periods leads to moulting problems, white muscle problems.
  • No remedy for viral diseases.
  • To avoid disease incident in culture one should have knowledge about reasons for disease incidence, identification of disease and remedial measures.
  • Weekly health check-up as well as bacterial load test is must.
  • Water quality parameters should always maintained in optimum range to avoid disease incidence.
  • Strict biosecurity measures to be observed throughout the culture i.e, physical, chemical and biological biosecurity measures for quality and high yields.
